Bringing Awareness to Those with Mental Health Issues

Learn More

Recognize signs of people going through a difficult time

  • Not acting normal
  • Loss of interest of things they used to enjoy
  • Talking about hopelessness
  • Being more reckless
  • Isolating from family and friends
  • Negative about life
  • Harming themselves

How to help others you suspect are going through mental health problems

  • Encourage people to talk themselves out - tell them to open their hearts up
  • Be supportive, compassionate and listen
  • Take a moment to hang out with them, talk, and reach out to them
  • Notice when a person is closing his or her walls
  • Keep lines of communication available
  • Open your door, go out, be around people

How to Approach Those With Mental Illness

“I feel like something’s up. Can you share with me?”
“Whenever you’re ready to talk, I’m ready to listen.”
“I know life can be overwhelming sometimes. So, if you need to talk, I’m here.”
“I’ve noticed you’ve been down lately. What’s going on?”

Things to do during social distancing to maintain good mental health

Stay connected - FaceTime, call, and text your friends
Meditation/breathing exercises - calms you down
Be kind - when you do something nice for someone else, your brain’s pleasure and reward centers light up. This is known as ‘helper’s high’.
See this quarantine as an opportunity - do things which you’ve wanted to do but never had the time to do.
